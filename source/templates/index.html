Data Driven Criminal Justice

{{$global}}

Surveys on the needs, challenges and variables of developing a data driven criminal justice system

The GovLab is leading a year-long project, funded by the John and Laura Arnold Foundation, and in close collaboration with the office of the U.S. Chief Data Scientist at the White House, to analyze and document how data flows through the criminal justice system. The aim is to identify policy and legal barriers to effective data sharing among agencies and between agencies and researchers.

To date the GovLab is conducting a series of ongoing surveys designed to provide a more complete picture and help us understanding of how criminal justice communities use data and where there is a need for more information and training, as well as a survey of empirical criminal justice researchers.
